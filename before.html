Before the revolution, Ethiopia faced crisis. In 1973, a famine struck the north. The government tried to hide the disaster, but the truth spread. Thousands died, and many saw the emperor’s silence as a sign of failure. Across cities and villages, the mood shifted. Trust in the monarchy weakened.

Young people began to speak out. College students led protests. Teachers and farmers joined them. Across the country, workers refused to show up for jobs that paid too little. These protests were not just about food or money. Many were influenced by global ideas. They wanted land reform, equality, and change.
