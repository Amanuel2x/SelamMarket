In 1974, Ethiopia entered a period of major change. After years of poverty and rising anger, protests broke out across the country. The government, led by Emperor Haile Selassie, was no longer able to respond to the needs of the people. Students marched in the streets. Workers went on strike. Families struggled through a famine while leaders lived in wealth. By September, the emperor had been arrested, and a new military council had taken control.

This project tells the story of how the Ethiopian Revolution began and what it left behind. It looks at the events that shaped the uprising and the ways everyday people were affected. Some stayed. Some fled. But all were changed by what happened. This history is still part of how Ethiopian families see the world today.

"We knew change was coming, but not like this." — Eyewitness, 1974
