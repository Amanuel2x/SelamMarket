In the months that followed, protest turned into rebellion. Groups organized in secret and planned strikes. In some cities, violence broke out. The military, tired of chaos, stepped in. A group of officers known as the Derg began to take control.

By August, soldiers had arrested top officials. On September 12, the emperor himself was removed from power. Cameras captured the moment. The image of Haile Selassie being led away shocked the world. Headlines ran stories about Ethiopia’s future. The Derg promised stability, but their rule soon brought fear.
