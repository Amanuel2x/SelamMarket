This history is not just told through facts. It lives in the memories of those who experienced it. Some remember hiding during raids. Others recall walking for days to escape the country. Many families split apart. Some never reunited.

There are differences in how people remember the revolution. Those who stayed often speak of fear and change. Refugees remember the journey out. Children of that time grew up fast. Today, younger generations ask questions. They try to understand what their parents and grandparents lived through. These stories help keep the memory alive.
